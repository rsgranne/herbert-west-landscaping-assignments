Course Description

Hopefully you saw this before you signed up for the course:

Course includes hands-on scripting of HTML5 and CSS3 to develop well-designed websites. Learn to enhance a sire with audio and visual clips, forms with HTML5 data validation, or CSS3 transitions. Use JavaScript and jQuery to add features like accordions, tabs, and slide shows to a page. Explore the principles of web design and Search Engine Optimization (SEO), as well as how to deploy your finished sites. Create Responsive Web Design so pages will look great and work correctly on any screen, from phone to tablet to desktop.

Course Objectives

By the end of this course, you will be able to:

  1. Gain familiarity with key concepts about the Internet, the Web, Web browsers, Web addresses, & the Browser Processing Pipeline
  2. Understand 50 important HTML elements, what they do, & how to use them
  3. Understand key CSS concepts, selectors, properties, & values, what they do, & how to use them
  4. Learn some basic concepts about JavaScript, including how to integrate it with your HTML
  5. Set up, configure, & use Visual Studio Code, a professional tool for editing webpages
  6. Learn about the important differences between various image, audio, & videos formats & when to use them
  7. Know what responsive web design is, why it’s vital, & how to apply it using Bootstrap
  8. Know what a domain name is & how to register one
  9. Understand the various options when it comes to hosting a website: operating systems, servers, hosting services, & how to upload & download files
  10. Create a complete website for a Canadian Lawn Care Company (that deals in mulch, goats, the uncanny, & eldritch horrors!) & that utilizes all the HTML, CSS, & JavaScript we cover in the course

Technical Needs & Skills

You need to use a laptop or desktop computer that runs a modern version of macOS, Windows, or Linux. I love my iPad, but tablets just aren’t there yet when it comes to Web development, so please don’t try to use one. Chromebooks are not great either.

Your computer needs to have a modern web browser installed:

  • a Chromium-based browser; this includes Chrome & the new Edge (pre-installed on newer releases of Windows), but I would recommend giving Brave a try — it’s just like Chrome & Edge, but without the corporate surveillance & data mining (supposedly Canvas works best in a Chromium-based browser, but if you like Firefox or Safari, try those & let me know)
  • Firefox
  • Safari comes pre-installed on all Macs
  • You do not need Lockdown browser, because I would never require you to use that insecure thing

You will need to install some free software, but I will walk you through that when it happens.

As far as technical skills, you should know how to do the following:

  • use your file manager (Finder on macOS & File Explorer on Windows) to create files & folders as well as copy & paste files & folders; in addition, you should understand how a folder structure works (files go in folders, which go in other folders, which go in other folders, & so on)

  • install software
  • use a Web browser
  • zip (compress or archive) & unzip (unarchive) files & folders
  • work with multiple programs, & multiple windows, open at the same time

Personal skills that are always good to have, but especially in this course, are:

Grading

Your grade will be based on the following factors:

  • Attendance (15%): This is a very intensive, hands-on course, and we will cover a lot of material, so it is imperative that you are in class. If you miss classes, you will fall behind, & we will need to talk about your grade.
  • Online Discussions (5%): There may be some online discussion questions that you will need to answer. Please note that to get full points on a discussion question, you need to provide an answer to my prompt & also respond to another student. Failure to do one of those means you will lose 50% of the grade for that discussion question.

Course Policies

HOMEWORK: I am not a stickler for due dates & times, because I know that life happens. However, do not fall behind with your homework. There are a lot of assignments in this course, and each one builds on the previous one, so you should not work on assignment D unless I have already graded assignments A through C. If you need an extension, just let me know & I will be cool with it. However, falling further & further behind will eventually be very problematic for both you & me, & will eventually reduce your grade on individual assignments.

Please note that I will not grade code by myself. Instead, I will grade all code with you. We can meet in person or we can meet over Zoom at a convenient time for you. Most of the time these will be very quick meetings. You will also know immediately what your grade is and what you need to do to improve.

AI: I cannot stop you from using ChatGPT & other generative AI tools, but since I will be grading all assignments with you, you can expect that I will quiz you about your code, especially if I suspect you are using AI tools & just copying & pasting without really understanding the code. The more you use AI tools to generate your code, the less you will understand, & while that may get you a decent grade in this course, it will spell your doom when you go into an interview & cannot answer even basic questions because you never learned how to code & only learned how to query an AI & then paste the answer it gave you. You may use AI to help you, but do not rely on it!

GRADING: Assignments will be graded as soon as we meet. It is up to you to contact me & propose a date & time for us to meet.
