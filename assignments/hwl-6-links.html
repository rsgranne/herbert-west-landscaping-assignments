Breadcrumb <nav>

Underneath our primary navigation will live our breadcrumb navigation. A reference to the trail of bread crumbs left by Hansel and Gretel (well, actually, it was Gretel; Hansel was just walking around with his mouth open looking at the squirrels) in the fairy tale, breadcrumb navigation on websites tells users what page they are on & how that page fits into the website‚Äôs structure, & also provides navigation within that immediate structure.

Here‚Äôs an example. Say you clicked on a link & ended up on a page with this at the top: Home > Creatures > Cthulhu (those are fake links, so don‚Äôt try to click on them). You would know several things from seeing that breadcrumb nav:

  1. You were currently on the Cthulhu page, because it‚Äôs the last page listed & it is not a link
  2. Cthulhu is a sub-page of Creatures, because of Creatures > Cthulhu
  3. Creatures is a sub-page of Home, because of Home > Creatures
  4. You could click on Creatures to jump up a level to read about all the Creatures on this website, including Cthulhu, because Creatures is a link
  5. If you clicked on Home, you would end up on the home page for the website, because Home is a link

The ordered list is going to eventually become that breadcrumb navigation, but for now, simply surround that list with <nav>. As I mentioned above, for accessibility reasons we need to label each <nav>, so this one will be <nav aria-label="breadcrumb">.
